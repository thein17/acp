A Necessity in the Digital Age

In today's society, where the digital landscape shapes how we learn, work, connect, and live, the internet is no longer a luxury but a necessity. The COVID-19 pandemic has emphasized the critical need for digital access, ensuring educational continuity, telehealth access, and the ability to work from home. The ACP has played a crucial role in ensuring that economic status does not bar access to these essential services. As the digital divide continues to widen, the Affordable Connectivity Program Extension Act of 2024 represents a crucial step towards promoting digital equity and ensuring that all Americans have access to the opportunities and resources the internet provides

The Affordable Connectivity Program Extension Act of 2024

To address this issue, the Affordable Connectivity Program Extension Act of 2024, has been introduced. This legislation aims to provide $7 billion in additional funding for the ACP, ensuring the program's continuity and preventing millions of households from losing their internet access.
The proposed extension has garnered widespread support from various organizations, including the National Association of Counties (NACo), the National Association of Regional Councils (NARC), and the Communications Workers of America, who recognize the ACP's vital role in promoting digital equity and enabling access to essential services.
We urge all citizens to voice their support for the Affordable Connectivity Program Extension Act of 2024. Together, we can ensure that the digital divide is bridged, and no one is left behind in the digital age. Your voice can make a difference in securing affordable internet access for millions of households across the nation.
